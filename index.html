Getting started

Some of Thailands most useful apps require switching to a Thai App Store or Google Play account. Follow these quick steps to create local profiles and unlock the full catalog.

Create a Thai Apple ID

  • 1Open Settings > Media & Purchases and sign out of your current Apple ID.
  • 2Create a new Apple ID at appleid.apple.com and choose Thailand as the region.
  • 3Use a Thai mailing address (hotel, serviced apartment, or coworking space works).
  • 4Sign in on your device with the new ID to download Thai-exclusive appsswitch back anytime.

Switch Google Play country

  • 1Open the Google Play Store, tap your profile, then Account > Country and profiles.
  • 2Select Thailand and add a Thai payment method (local card or SIM card often works).
  • 3Browse againThai-only apps will now appear. Consider creating a dedicated travel Google account since country changes are limited to once per year.

Tips before you download

These quick tips will save you timeand a few bahtonce you're on the ground in Thailand.

PromptPay is everywhere QR payments are accepted at convenience stores, street food stalls, and even taxis. Set up at least one wallet that supports PromptPay.
Offline prep pays off Download offline maps and translation packs before island hopping or visiting rural provinces with spotty coverage.
Expect verification hurdles Some financial apps require a Thai phone number or ID. Plan ahead if you rely on international SIMs.
Keep dual accounts Many expats maintain separate Apple/Google accounts for their home country and Thailand to avoid annual region limits.
